What is Lync?

Lync is an internal communications platform written by Microsoft and provides capabilities of conferencing and IM (Instant Messaging), as well as having the ability of intergrating the into other office programs such as Outlook.

Master those keyboard shortcuts
Some keyboard shortcuts for the most commonly used features in Lync.

  • Windows logo key+A: Accept an incoming invitation notification.
  • Windows log key+Esc: Decline an invitation notification.

    • In themain windowthe following shortcuts can be applied:

  • Ctrl+1: Go to the Contact list tab.
  • Ctrl+2: Go to the Activity Feeds tab.
  • Ctrl+3: Go to the Conversation list tab.
  • Ctrl+4: Go to the Phone tab.
  • Alt+A: View all conversations when in the Conversation list tab.
  • Alt+D: View missed conversations when in Conversation list tab.
  • Alt+C: View missed calls when in Conversation list tab.

Adding (Pinning Contacts)
On the Lync main window begin typing the name of a user (their first name followed by last name, or email address).
Once the user you are after becomes listed, it is now possible toadd the user to your contacts.


A user may be added to your contacts in 2 forms:

  1. In to a specific group, by selecting Add to new group
  2. To All Contacts

Changing your status
Being an instant messaging platform, showing your status and availability to other users (fellow colleagues is very important)
Under your profile, located at the top of the main window with your name and profile picture, is a place to set your status.
Click on the drop down arrow next to your current status, to show the other status options available.
Click a status, to set your current status.

Adding people to a conversation
Adding colleagues to a conference call: Drag a contact from theLync main windowand drop it into theConversation windowto add the person to the conference call.

The lync lobby
The virtual lobby in Lync is used when you setup conference calls. This lobby allows people who call into the meeting to know that they are in the right spot, and you (or the creator of the conversation) to act as the "receptionist" to decide who can get into the call, and who can not.
Once you have set up a new meeting, navigate to Meeting Options on the Home tab, and select the meeting options you want.
Once people dial in, you will be notified that there are people in the lobby waiting, and will be able to control the attendance.

Remove background bother
Background noise (such as barking dogs, outside voices) may make it diffcult to hear the speaker properly during meetings. Thecall leaderhas the ability to not only see who is speaking but to also remove this background noise by muting the whole conversation, or a single user.
To put the whole conversation on mute: In the conversation, click on the People Options menu.
To put a single user on mute: On the participant list, right-click the participant which you wisht to put on mute and then select either mute or unmute.

Transferring a call
The others won't hear a beep or a tone click the Transfer menu, then either select your other phone numbers, or transfer to somebody else by selecting Another Person or Number and select the contact which you wish to transfer to.

Be notified when a colleague coming online
Right click on a contact
Click Tage for Status Change Alerts
This will notify you when this user changes their status to "available", allowing you to get in touch with them

Finding experts
Stumped on a subject matter? Needed help with something specific. Lync will help you find experts who have matching skills in their SharePoint profile, making it easy to make contact when in need of assistance.
On the Lync main window begin typing keywords into the search box, be sure to select skill to search by skill and Lync will do the rest.

* An important point to note is that these skill sets will be searched using the information provided on the SharePoint profile for the user.

Activity update feed
"Out to a client site visit" Use Lync to let your follow colleagues know where you are. The Activity update feed on Lync allows everyone to stay informed about important milestones; such as news and organisation changed.
The activity update feed is accessed by the button menu next to your contacts, and clicking on it will allow you to check the latest status of your contacts.

Open meetings in the web browser
When you receive a Meeting invite from someone for a Lync meeting, it typically looks like

https://meet.modalitysystems.com/vikas.jaswal/V544444

When you click on this it launches your Lync client. Now assume for whatever reason you do not want to use the Lync client to join the meeting. You can also use the browser to join the meeting . To do this open your browser, paste the meeting link and add?sl=1to end of the meeting. So the meeting invite will now look like:

https://meet.modalitysystems.com/vikas.jaswal/V544444?sl=1

which will be able to copied and pasted to open in your web browser.

Speed dial into a conversation
Quickly join into meetings by setting up speed dialing with Lync using the following steps: You will ultimately create a new speed-dial entry with a number of the form:

18001231234,,,{confID+#},,,*,*,{yourExtensionOrFullPhoneNumber+#},,{yourPIN+#}

where;

  • 18001231234is the Conferencing attendant access number. You are able to find this by creating a new online meeting and inspecting the body of the invite for a phone number.
  • {confID+#}is the Conference ID of the meeting. This can also be found by creating a new meeting and inspecting the body for aconference ID. Note that the conference ID which you will be using will not change between meetings, unless you are wanting to join someone elses' meeting, in which case you will use their conference ID.
  • **these 2 asterisks are necessary in order to instruct the system, that you wish to manually authenticate your self with aphone numberand PIN.
  • {yourExtensionOrFullPhoneNumber+#}this can be either your extension, or your full phone number (starting with country code). If you’re not sure, try dialing up manually first and entering your phone number and PIN to see if it works. Again, make sure you end with ‘#’.
  • {yourPIN+#} – this is your conferencing PIN, the same PIN you use on your deskphone (Lync Phone Edition) if you have one. If you’re not sure what your PIN is (or you never set one!), then make a new online meeting and click the“Forgot your dial-in PIN?”link in the body of the invite. You’ll find yourself on the dialin-conferencing webpage where you can change your PIN.

Copying email addresses
You can copy an email address directly off your Lync contact list for single or multiple userswithout opening each Contact Card.
To copy a single contact:, right click on the contact name and selectcopyfrom the menu.

Copy multiple contacts selectively (to do this press the Ctrl key while you click on the contacts you want to copy, and then right click)

Copy users from all the groups from your Contact list To do this just collapse all your groups, then press Shift and while you are holding it click the first group and the last group. All the groups should be highlighted now. Just right click and select Copy.

Now what you get after a copy (paste into a notepad), something like this:
joe@allthingsuc.co.uk; jim@allthingsuc.co.uk;kate@contoso.com; bill@fabrikam;stella@allthingsuc.co.uk; vik@modalitysystems.com; lisa@outlook.com.

Change sound settings
Now I am not talking here about changing your ringtone which you can quite easily do by going into Options>Ringtones and Sounds but all other sounds you hear when you are using Lync – e.g.:what sounds is played when someone sends and IM to you, when the call disconnects, when the call is connecting or ending, etc.Unsurprisingly, as Lync client runs on top of Windows it’s at this level where you configure these sounds. Go to Control Panel then select Sounds and go to the Sounds tab. From here scroll until you find Lync. Just underneath it you will find all kinds of Lync Client sounds which you can change to your heart’s content.

Change your inactive timeout status
Change your status so that it does not change to Inactive automatically after 5 minutes. Change it to something higher….30…60…100…up to 360, whatever you like.
Navigate to Lync Options and select the Status option.
